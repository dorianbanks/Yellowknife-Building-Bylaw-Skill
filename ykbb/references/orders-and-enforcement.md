# Orders and Enforcement — Workflow (§§ 38–44)

Use this guide when the question is about **orders issued by an
Inspector** — what types, how they're served, how they take effect,
and how they can be reviewed.

---

## Order types — §38 (pp. 17–19)

§38 opens with the general authority:

> An Inspector may order an applicant or a person who contravenes this
> By-law to comply with its provisions **within a period of time
> specified in the order**. If the applicant or person does not
> comply within the specified time, the City may take a specified
> action or measure at the expense of the applicant or person subject
> to the order.

There are **six order types** (§38(1)):

### (a) Order to "Uncover Work"

Trigger — any of:

- work carried out without a permit;
- work covered without inspection being completed; or
- reasonable grounds to believe work not in compliance with By-law or
  permit conditions.

### (b) Order to "Stop-Work"

Effect — the owner and every person shall **cease all work
immediately** and perform no further work until the Inspector
rescinds the order in writing. Exceptions:

- work necessary to carry out another Inspector order;
- work required to make the work **safe**.

### (c) Order of "Unsafe Condition"

Trigger — work determined unsafe because of non-compliance, faulty
work, dilapidation, unsoundness of structural strength, open /
unguarded condition, abandonment, or any other reason.

Inspector may order repair, demolition, or any other action to
eliminate the unsafe condition. **All costs are the sole
responsibility** of the applicant / person to whom the order was
issued.

### (d) Order to "Do Not Occupy"

Trigger — occupancy in contravention of the By-law (typically,
occupancy without an OC). The Inspector posts the order visibly. The
owner and every person shall **cease occupancy immediately**.

### (e) Order for "Removal of Unauthorized Encroachment"

Inspector may order removal of any unauthorized encroachment onto the
property line.

### (f) Order for "Removal of Structure"

Inspector may order removal of any structure constructed in
contravention of the By-law.

## Administrative rules — §§ 39–43

| § | Rule |
|---|---|
| §39 | Order must be on the prescribed form (set by City Manager) |
| §40 | Inspector posts a copy **at the work**; no one may remove or alter without written Inspector authorization. **Order is effective when posted.** |
| §41 | Orders served personally on the applicant or other affected person |
| §42 | Once issued, no person shall contravene any aspect except: (a) work necessary to comply with / make safe per the By-law; or (b) work authorized by the Inspector in writing |
| §43 | Applicant is responsible for costs of complying, including costs of satisfying the Inspector that the order has been complied with |

## Review of Order — §44 (pp. 19–20)

A person who receives a written order may request **Council review** of
the order.

### Filing requirements (§44(2))

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Form | Written |
| Fee | Per the Fees and Charges By-law |
| Contents | Reasons for review and relief sought; summary of relevant facts; requester contact info |
| Delivery | To the **City Clerk** within **14 days** after receiving the order |
| Hearing | By Council at a regular or special meeting **within 21 days** of the City Clerk receiving the request |

### Council decision (§44(3))

> Council may confirm, vary, replace or cancel the order. **Council's
> decision may not contravene the N.B.C.**

### Service (§44(4))

Council serves a copy of its decision on the requester within **14
days** of making it.

### Imminent danger override (§44(5))

Where Council, the City Manager, the Director of Planning and
Development, or another authorized person believes imminent danger to
public health or safety exists, they may:

- **shorten** the time specified in the order; or
- **initiate action** to remedy and eliminate the danger **before**
  the review is heard.

## Offences and Penalties — §§ 45–46 (pp. 20–21)

### Summary Conviction fines (§45)

Any person who contravenes a provision is guilty of an offence,
liable on Summary Conviction to a fine not exceeding:

| Party | Maximum fine | Imprisonment (in default of fine) |
|---|---:|---|
| Individual | **$2,000** | up to 6 months |
| Corporation | **$10,000** | — |

### General offences (§46(1))

Every person who does any of the following is guilty of an offence:

- knowingly provides false or misleading information in any
  application or statement;
- fails to comply with any order, direction, or other requirement;
- contravenes any provision of the By-law.

### Strict liability (§46(2))

> Any offence created under this By-law shall be considered to be a
> **strict liability** offence.

Practical consequence — the prosecution need not prove intent; due
diligence is the available defence.

### Summary Offence Tickets — Voluntary Fines (§46(3) + Schedule A)

An Officer may issue a **Summary Offence Ticket Information** for any
By-law violation. In lieu of prosecution, the person may pay the
**Voluntary Penalty** set out in Schedule A:

| Voluntary offence | Individual | Corporation |
|---|---:|---:|
| Failure to comply with an order | $200 | $1,000 |
| Failure to display permit | $50 | $50 |
| Failure to obtain a permit | $200 | $1,000 |
| Failure to comply with permit conditions | $500 | $500 |

### Continuing offence (§46(4))

A contravention constitutes a **separate offence for each day or part
of a day** on which it continues.

### Joint and several liability (§46(5))

Where more than one owner — each is jointly and severally liable for
fines, fees, or charges under this By-law.

## Drafting guidance

### When advising a client who's been issued an order

1. **Read the order carefully.** Posting triggers effect (§40(2)) —
   the clock is running.
2. **Note the 14-day window to request Council review** (§44(2)d).
   Draft the review request immediately if contesting.
3. **Don't touch the work** except to comply or make safe (§42) —
   any other work is a separate contravention.
4. **Track costs** — they're the applicant's under §43, including
   compliance-demonstration costs.
5. **If there's an imminent-danger flag** (§44(5)), Council / City
   Manager can shorten timelines or act before the review — expect
   this on fire, structural, or life-safety issues.

### When advising a client considering a permit shortcut

Remind them about:

- §51 — double fees if work starts before permit;
- Schedule A — $200/$1,000 voluntary fines for failure to obtain a
  permit;
- §45 — Summary Conviction up to $2,000 individual / $10,000
  corporation;
- §46(4) — per-day offences (the clock keeps compounding until
  compliance);
- §46(2) — strict liability (intent doesn't matter).

It usually pencils out cheaper to wait for the permit.
