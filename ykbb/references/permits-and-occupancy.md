# Permits and Occupancy — Workflow (§§ 17–24)

Use this guide when the question is about **getting or keeping a
building permit** in Yellowknife — who needs one, what triggers an
exemption, how permits are issued, and when an occupancy certificate
(OC) is required.

---

## Is a permit required? — §17 Exemptions

A permit is NOT required (§17, subject to Inspector discretion) for:

1. **Infrastructure**, except the portion within 1 m of a structure
   *(note: water/sewer connections to mains still require a permit
   under the Water and Sewer Services By-law — §17(1)a)*.
2. **Temporary field office** used directly on an active work site
   (must be removed on work completion).
3. **Accessory buildings ≤ 10 m²**.
4. **Landscaping** where excavation or fill does not exceed 600 mm
   over existing grade.
5. **Residential decks, patios, and retaining walls** where the top of
   the platform is ≤ 600 mm above finished grade.
6. **Minor mechanical repair** — valve/faucet/fixture/service water
   heater replacement with similar equipment, blockage cleared, or
   leak repaired, with **no piping change**.

§18 transitional rule — the by-law does not apply to lawful permit
applications received by Building Services before the effective date.

---

## Permit required — §19

> It is an offence under this By-law to commence work prior to a
> permit being issued. (§19)

Additional permits may be required if the Inspector determines more
are necessary for compliance.

### What the permit does NOT do (§20)

Issuance does not:
- relieve the applicant of sole responsibility for compliance;
- constitute a representation, warranty, or assurance that any
  requirement has been complied with.

### Prohibited acts during work (§21)

- tampering with notices, orders, permits, certificates;
- doing work substantially at variance with accepted plans without
  written Inspector approval;
- submitting false or misleading information;
- contravening permit conditions / notices / orders;
- refusing entry, obstructing, or interfering with an Inspector /
  Officer.

## Permit issuance — §22

| § | Topic | Key points |
|---|---|---|
| §22(1) | **Refusal** | incomplete info; effective date of development permit not reached; work not permitted by any other by-law |
| §22(2) | **Issuance** | complete application + fees paid → permit issued |
| §22(3) | **Conditions** | Inspector may impose conditions necessary for compliance |
| §22(4) | **Partial permit** | may issue for portion of work before all plans accepted; applicant proceeds *at their own risk* |
| §22(5) | **Foundation-only restore** | if only foundation built and no further permit issued, applicant must restore site within 60 days of order |
| §22(6) | **Expiry** | **2 years** from issuance |
| §22(7) | **Extension** | by written request for adverse weather / strikes / material shortages / similar hardship beyond applicant's control |
| §22(8) | **Revocation** | infringement of condition / issued in error / based on false info |

## Permit applications — §23

| § | Topic |
|---|---|
| §23(1) | Online application via City's software; Agent must submit authorized agent form |
| §23(2) | Application must include legal description, detailed work description, **occupancy classification**, and any other info required by the Inspector |
| §23(3) | Site plans must reference an **up-to-date survey** showing all structures and locations on site |
| §23(4) | Inspector may require **RDP** involvement for work involving NBC Parts 3, 4, 5, or 6, or as required by the By-law — with letters of assurance. Information must be provided in **digital format**. |
| §23(5) | **Demolition Permits** — no permit issued and no demolition until all municipal taxes and utility levies on the property are paid |
| §23(6) | Revisions may require re-submittal |
| §23(7) | **Abandoned** — permit deemed abandoned and information may be destroyed if work sits idle for **180 days** |

## Occupancy Certificate (OC) — §24

Trigger: work is substantially complete and **ready for occupancy**.

### Process

1. Applicant applies to Building Services Division for an OC before
   any occupancy takes place (§24(1)).
2. **No person shall occupy the work without an OC** (§24(2)).
3. OC issuance criteria (§24(3)) — all of the following:
   - Required letters of assurance submitted;
   - Life-safety equipment functioning and successfully tested;
   - RDP reports submitted to the Inspector;
   - The work can be used for its intended purpose.
4. **Partial OC** (§24(4)) — available for self-contained work with
   essential services that meets the By-law for that part.
5. **Incomplete-documentation OC** (§24(5)) — Inspector may issue an
   OC if substantially complete, with a **refundable deposit + admin
   fee**. Deposit returned if docs submitted within 90 days; forfeited
   otherwise.

### Drafting guidance

When helping a client prepare for OC:
- Confirm **all life-safety commissioning** (fire alarm, sprinkler,
  emergency lighting, egress hardware) is documented.
- Verify **letters of assurance** from every RDP of record — Schedule B
  and Schedule C under NBC 2.2.7 if referenced.
- If any deficiencies remain, negotiate a §24(5) partial/deposit OC
  early rather than delaying move-in — factor the deposit + admin into
  the schedule.

## Common scenarios

### "Can I put up a 9 m² garden shed without a permit?"

§17(3) exempts accessory buildings ≤ 10 m². 9 m² is exempt — but the
Inspector retains discretion (§17 lead). Structural / foundation /
zoning / development-permit rules may still apply under other
by-laws.

### "My client added a 100 mm concrete slab extension to their deck, which is 500 mm above grade."

§17(5) exempts residential decks ≤ 600 mm above grade. No building
permit required **for the deck itself**. But if the extension ties
into the house structure (ledger board, new roof, etc.) that work is
not exempt.

### "The inspector is asking for a Real Property Report — do I really have to provide that?"

§29(1)(i) — yes. Applicant shall provide an up-to-date Real Property
Report to the Building Services Division **prior to occupancy being
issued**.

### "My permit is 18 months old and the foundation's in. I'm not ready to build yet."

§22(6) — 2 years total. You have 6 months. Request an extension
(§22(7)) in writing with a hardship reason, or anticipate the §22(5)
60-day site-restore order if it lapses.
