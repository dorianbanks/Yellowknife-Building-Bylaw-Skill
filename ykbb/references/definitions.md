# Defined Terms — §3 (pp. 3–8)

§2 establishes the interpretation rule: **terms used in this By-law have
the meanings prescribed in the National Building Code** unless defined
below. §3 then lists the by-law-specific definitions.

Order of definitions follows the PDF exactly. Page numbers refer to

## Useful to know

- **NBC-fallback rule (§2).** If a term is not defined in §3, use the NBC
  definition. This is the pivot that imports the NBC's entire
  terminology into this by-law — including Part 3 / Part 9 scoping,
  occupancy classifications (Groups A–F), fire separations, and so on.
- **"RDP" is NWT-specific.** NAPEG or NWTAA, not BC/Alberta equivalents.
  Out-of-territory firms must obtain NWTAA temporary/occasional licensure
  or NAPEG equivalent before their seal will be accepted.
- **"Existing Building" has a 5-year floor.** Renovations to buildings
  that have existed for **less than** five years can't lean on
  "Existing Building" provisions of the by-law or NBC Part 11 — treat
  them as new construction for compliance purposes.
- **"Multi-Family Dwelling" threshold is "more than two" units.** §61
  MFD sprinkler rule (≥3 units → NFPA 13) follows this definition.
- **"Minor Addition" is ≤10% floor surface area.** This is tighter than
  some jurisdictions' "minor" threshold — check scope before claiming
  it.
