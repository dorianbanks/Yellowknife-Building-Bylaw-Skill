# Responsibilities — Workflow (§§ 29–35)

Use this guide when the question is **who must do what** — applicant
obligations, RDP requirements, Inspector powers, liability exclusions,
and the City's intervention authority.

---

## Applicant — §29 (p. 14)

§29(1) opens with a categorical assignment:

> It is the **full and sole** responsibility of the applicant to carry
> out the work in respect of which the permit was issued in compliance
> with this By-law.

## Registered Design Professionals (RDPs) — §30 (p. 15)

### Definition recap (§3)

An RDP is an engineer in good standing with **NAPEG** or an architect
in good standing with the **NWTAA** — entitled to practice in the NWT.
Out-of-territory practitioners cannot simply stamp; they need NWTAA
temporary/occasional licensure (architects) or NAPEG equivalent.

### Inspector may require (§30(1))

Where the proposed work requires specialized knowledge, an Inspector
may require RDP involvement for:

- Plans and related documents **prepared, signed and sealed** by RDPs
  with **letters of assurance** and any other supporting documentation.
- **Field reviews** performed by RDPs or their designate, submitted to
  the Inspector.

§23(4) reinforces this for NBC **Parts 3, 4, 5, or 6** work specifically.

### RDP obligations (§30(2))

An RDP who designs the work or part thereof shall:

- ensure the design complies with the By-law;
- coordinate (with applicant and Inspector) the review and acceptance
  of any post-issuance design changes;
- provide all required **letters of assurance** to the Inspector.

### Field reviews (§30(3))

> An RDP or their designate who undertakes a field review of the work
> shall inspect the work at **intervals appropriate to determine
> general compliance** with the design and permit.

### Reliance — Inspector may rely on letters of assurance (§30(4))

> Where letters of assurance have been provided, an Inspector may rely
> **solely** on the letters as confirmation that the design and work
> comply with the permit and this By-law.

The Inspector retains discretion to attend the work to complete an
inspection and give direction (§30(4)b).

## Inspectors — §§ 31–33 (pp. 15–16)

### Powers (§31(1))

An Inspector may:

- exercise the powers set out in this By-law;
- give **written directions, decisions and orders** requiring
  correction of work done in contravention;
- **enter** on any land or work at any reasonable time to ascertain
  compliance — except where a structure is occupied (then §37
  applies).

### Inspector qualifications — three classes (§32)

| Class | Scope |
|---|---|
| **Class 1** | One- and two-unit dwellings |
| **Class 2** | Any structure under NBC **Part 9** (includes Class 1) |
| **Class 3** | All structures under the NBC (includes Classes 1 and 2) |

An Inspector certified in another province or territory may perform
plan reviews and inspections in Yellowknife within their class level,
or as additionally assigned on hire (§32(1)).

### Identification (§33)

Each Inspector shall carry ID establishing their position, produced
on request.

## Liability — §34 (p. 16)

> **No proceeding may be commenced in any court** against the Manager
> of Building Services Division or an Inspector for anything done or
> omitted in **good faith** by them in exercising their powers or
> carrying out their duties under this By-law.

Good faith is the operative test. Bad faith / malfeasance falls outside
this protection.

---

## City intervention powers — §35 (p. 16)

The City may take measures it considers necessary to protect the
public:

- **Unsafe condition** — where due notice to correct has not been
  complied with, the City may, at the applicant's expense:
  - demolish / remove / make safe;
  - take any other protective measure.
- **Imminent danger** — where immediate measures are required to
  avoid imminent danger of fire or accident, the City may act
  **without notice** at the applicant's expense.

## Enforcement: Entry — §§ 36–37 (p. 17)

### §36 — Inspector may (in enforcement)

- enter the work (or adjoining structures/premises for inspection);
- be assisted by a person with special or expert knowledge;
- require production of any relevant document;
- conduct tests, inquiries, take samples / measurements / photos /
  video;
- require the applicant to take and supply tests and samples **at
  their expense**.

### §37 — Entry of occupied part

Despite §36, an Inspector **shall not enter an occupied part of a
structure** except:

- with the occupant's consent;
- where exigent circumstances exist;
- with 24-hour notice to the property owner; or
- where imminent and serious danger to public health or safety
  requires City action.

## Drafting guidance

- **Describe the Applicant as the primary contract party.** The by-law
  names the Applicant (owner or Agent) as singularly responsible. The
  RDP is an agent-of-care whose letter of assurance lets the Inspector
  take the work at face value — it does not transfer statutory
  liability away from the Applicant.
- **Letters of assurance are structural.** §30(4) lets the Inspector
  rely *solely* on them. Treat them with the weight that implies.
  Design an internal process for who stamps what and when.
- **Inspector classes matter for scoping.** A one-storey daycare is
  Part 9 — Class 2 Inspector is sufficient. A 4-storey mixed-use
  needs Class 3. Ask the City who's been assigned.
- **NWTAA temporary licensure lead time.** If your firm is
  out-of-territory, secure NWTAA licensure (and NAPEG for structural
  / mechanical / electrical engineers) before starting permit
  submissions — the RDP stamp has to be valid **when the letter of
  assurance is dated**.
