# Part 2 — Local Construction Requirements (§§ 53–70)

Use this guide for the **Yellowknife construction deltas** that layer
on top of the NBC. §53 makes the framing explicit:

> Part 2 outlines the local construction requirements **in addition
> to** the requirements in the N.B.C.

Nothing in Part 2 replaces the NBC. Everything in Part 2 *adds to* or
*exceeds* the NBC (remember §15 paramountcy — by-law prevails where it
exceeds the NBC).

## Foundations

See `foundations-retaining.md` for the full workflow (§§ 54–58 + Schedule B).

Summary:

- **§54** — continuous concrete foundation pinned to bedrock for a
  **Part 9 building** may be approved **without an RDP**, subject to
  rebar / dowel / tie / wall-height prescription (max wall height
  **3.66 m**).
- **§56** — concrete pier foundation pinned to bedrock for a **Part 9
  building** may be approved **without an RDP** if designed per
  **Schedule B**.
- **§57** — **Surface Mount Foundation** (woodblocks / concrete pads /
  ≥ 100 mm concrete slab) permitted without RDP if supporting a
  non-residential one-storey garage or accessory < 55 m² that is not
  masonry; concrete 30 MPa / 28d / 5–8% air; 10M rebar @ 305 mm o/c
  both ways; 40 mm cover.
- **§58** — **all other foundation types** → designed per NBC Part 4
  by an RDP with letters of assurance.

## Standpipe System (§59)

All standpipe systems shall:

- be designed per **NFPA 14** "Installation of Standpipe and Hose
  Systems" and this By-law;
- include an **approved 65 mm Straight Iron Pipe Thread (SIPT)** fire
  department connection **or 8 threads per inch**;
- be designed by an **RDP with stamped and sealed drawings,
  specifications, and letters of assurance** required for plan
  examination.

## Sprinkler System (§60)

Who may design:

| Building | Permitted designer |
|---|---|
| **Part 9** | Certified Sprinkler System Designer (CSSD), CWBSP, **or RDP** |
| **Part 3** | **RDP only** — with sealed, stamped drawings, hydraulic calculation, and applicable letters of assurance |

All designs must demonstrate compliance with the applicable **NFPA**
standard(s) and the **NBC**. For Part 9, confirmation of designer
qualification must be provided at submission.

## Multi-Family Dwelling (§61)

Two simple rules, with a Yellowknife-specific threshold:

### §61(1) — Sprinkler required at 3 units

> All multi-family dwellings **three units or more** must have a
> sprinkler system installed.

This is **tighter** than the NBC's generic MFD sprinkler threshold —
flag it every time when scoping a small-apartment / fourplex / sixplex
Yellowknife project.

### §61(2) — NFPA 13 for MFDs including 4+ storeys

> Sprinkler systems in all multi-family dwellings, **including four
> storeys and higher** in building height, must be designed,
> installed, and tested to comply with **NFPA Standard 13**.

NFPA 13 — not 13R / 13D. Residential-only standards (13R / 13D) are
not a permitted compliance path for MFDs in Yellowknife.

## Hydrants

### §62 — Hydrant outlet specifications

All hydrants shall be equipped with:

- **4-inch** outlet; and
- **Two 2½-inch** outlets;

All with **National Standard Thread** — and compatible with Yellowknife
Fire Department requirements. Coordinate with YKFD early on site
design; non-standard thread has delayed turnover on prior projects.

### §63 — Wall hydrants excluded from FDC distance

> Wall hydrants will not be considered a water source and will not be
> used to calculate the distance for Fire Department Connections.
> This requirement applies to both a standpipe system and sprinkler
> system connections.

Practical effect — when sizing/placing the FDC, the distance-to-source
calculation must rely on a **yard hydrant**, not a wall hydrant.

## Energy Efficiency (§64)

See `energy-efficiency.md` for the full prescriptive / performance
tables. Summary:

| Building type | Reference | Route |
|---|---|---|
| **Part 9 residential** | §64(1)–(5) + Schedule C | Prescriptive (Table 1) OR Performance (Table 2) |
| Part 9 residential factory-built temporary worker accommodation | §64(3) | Exempt if CSA-certified + meets minimum at date of construction |
| **Part 9 non-residential** | §64(6) | Comply with **NBC** |
| **Part 3 residential** | §64(7) + Schedule D | Comply with **Schedule D** (Yellowknife overlay) |
| **Part 3 non-residential** | §64(8) | Comply with **NECB** |

Yellowknife's Part 9 residential prescriptive values (Table 1) **exceed**
NBC 9.36 climate-zone defaults — always compare and apply the tighter
value under §15.

## Mechanical System (§65)

> Unless a new mechanical system or alterations to an existing
> mechanical system for a Part 9 building can be designed per Part 9
> of the N.B.C., subject to the Inspector's discretion, the Inspector
> is authorized to require the involvement of an RDP in the system
> design or alterations.

Translation:

- Straightforward Part 9 mechanical → may proceed per NBC Part 9.
- Anything non-standard / complex → Inspector can require an RDP
  (mechanical engineer registered with NAPEG).

## Water and Sewer (§66)

> The design shall comply with the City's **Water and Sewer Services
> By-law**, as amended, and this By-law.

That by-law is separate — pull it from the City when scoping any
project with a service connection or trucked service system (defined
in §3, p. 7).

## Retaining Wall

See `foundations-retaining.md` for the full workflow (§§ 67–69 +
Schedule E).

Threshold summary:

| Retaining wall height | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **≤ 600 mm**, not supporting a structure | **No permit required** (§67) |
| **> 600 mm and < 1200 mm**, not supporting a structure | Designed to prevent sliding/overturning; drawings per **Schedule E** (§68) |
| **> 1200 mm** OR supporting any structure above | **RDP-designed**, stamped / sealed structural drawings + letters of assurance (§69) |

## Signs (§70)

> Unless otherwise permitted by an inspector, all sign structures
> shall be designed per **Part 4 of the N.B.C.**

Part 4 is structural. Substantive sign scoping still needs to clear
the City's sign / zoning by-laws (separate instruments).

## Drafting guidance

- **Always check the NBC first, then apply Part 2 on top.** The
  by-law doesn't replace the NBC; §15 paramountcy means it only
  exceeds it.
- **Pinned-to-bedrock is the Yellowknife norm** — it's explicit
  in §§ 54 and 56 as the default small-building foundation.
  Surface Mount (§57) is narrow (non-residential, one storey,
  < 55 m²). Anything else goes to §58 (RDP + NBC Part 4).
- **For MFDs, the 3-unit sprinkler threshold is the most commonly
  missed Yellowknife-specific rule.** Add it to your project-start
  checklist.
- **Mechanical scope creep.** §65 gives the Inspector wide
  discretion to pull in an RDP. If the Part 9 mechanical is
  non-standard (commercial HRV, complex zoning), budget for it.
