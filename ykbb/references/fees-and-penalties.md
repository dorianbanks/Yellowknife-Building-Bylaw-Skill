# Fees and Penalties — Workflow (§§ 45–51 + Schedule A)

Quick reference for **money questions** — permit fees, refunds,
re-inspection charges, double fees, voluntary fines, and Summary
Conviction maximums.

---

## Permit fees — §§ 47–51 (p. 21)

### §47 — Permit fees

Permit fees are calculated **per the Fees and Charges By-law** (a
separate City of Yellowknife by-law, not reproduced here) and must be
**paid in full before any permit is issued**.

### §48 — Construction Value validation

Where permit fees are calculated on construction value, the Inspector
may use **current market value** to validate the applicant's declared
value. Translation — don't under-declare; the Inspector can adjust.

### §49 — Refunds

Where the applicant makes a **written request** to cancel a permit
application:

| Stage | Refund |
|---|---|
| Permit not issued, no inspection | Full refund **less the administration fee** |
| Permit issued, **no work begun** | **One-half** of the fee |
| Work begun **or** inspection completed | **No refund** |

### §50 — Re-inspection fee

Where work **fails** an inspection due to non-compliance, OR where an
inspection is requested but **work is not at a stage ready** for
inspection, a re-inspection charge may apply — at the discretion of
the Manager of Building Services, per the Fees and Charges By-law.

### §51 — Double fees

> At the discretion of the Manager of Building Services, **permit
> fees may be doubled** whenever any work occurs before a permit is
> issued, and additional fines may be applied per Schedule A —
> except where the work is required in emergency circumstances and
> is authorized by the City.

Cheap lesson — the permit route is almost always less expensive than
the "build first, permit later" route once the double-fees + Schedule
A voluntary fine + potential Summary Conviction stack is counted.

## Summary Conviction penalties — §45 (p. 20)

Any person who contravenes any provision is guilty of an offence,
liable on Summary Conviction to a fine not exceeding:

| Party | Max fine | Imprisonment in default |
|---|---:|---|
| Individual | **$2,000.00** | up to **6 months** |
| Corporation | **$10,000.00** | — |

---

## Voluntary Fines — Schedule A (p. 28)

A person issued a Summary Offence Ticket (by an Officer under §46(3))
may, **in lieu of prosecution**, pay the Voluntary Penalty:

| Offence | Individual | Corporation |
|---|---:|---:|
| (1) Failure to comply with an order | $200 | $1,000 |
| (2) Failure to display permit | $50 | $50 |
| (3) Failure to obtain a permit | $200 | $1,000 |
| (4) Failure to comply with permit conditions | $500 | $500 |

Voluntary fine is the *discount* alternative to Summary Conviction;
paying it avoids prosecution.

---

## Continuing and multiple offences — §§ 46(4)–(5)

### Per-day continuing (§46(4))

A contravention constitutes a **separate offence for each day or
part of a day** on which it continues. Every day of non-compliance
is independently fineable.

### Joint and several (§46(5))

Whenever fines / fees / charges are payable and there is more than
one owner, **each is jointly and severally liable** — the City can
collect the whole amount from any one of them.

---

## Strict liability — §46(2)

By-law offences are **strict liability**: the prosecution does not
need to prove intent. Due diligence is the defence. Practical
implication for advice — document your process. If you can show the
client took reasonable steps (retained an RDP, verified scope,
pulled permits on time, answered Inspector directions), the due
diligence defence is available.

## Quick cost-stack worst case

Scenario: client builds a 30 m² unpermitted addition; City discovers it
before OC; Inspector issues Stop-Work and Do-Not-Occupy orders; client
ignores orders for 14 days before responding.

| Item | Reference | Amount |
|---|---|---:|
| Failure to obtain a permit (voluntary) | Schedule A(3) | $200 – $1,000 |
| Failure to comply with order × 14 days (voluntary) | Schedule A(1) × §46(4) | up to 14 × $1,000 |
| Double permit fees | §51 | 2× normal fee |
| Cost of compliance (uncover work, tests, RDP) | §43 | actual |
| Possible Summary Conviction | §45 | up to $2,000 / $10,000 |

Point: not hypothetical. §46(4)'s per-day multiplier is the amplifier
people underestimate.

## Drafting guidance

- **Scope the permit fee early.** §47 references the Fees and Charges
  By-law — pull the current schedule from the City before finalising
  client budget.
- **If the client asks "what if we just start?"** — walk them through
  §51 + Schedule A + §45 + §46(4). The compounding math answers
  itself.
- **For a potentially out-of-scope variance mid-job**, use §22(4)
  partial permit or §22(3) conditions written in — rather than
  pushing forward and getting hit with §38 Stop-Work + §51.
