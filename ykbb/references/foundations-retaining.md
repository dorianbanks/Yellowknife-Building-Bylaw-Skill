# Foundations and Retaining Walls — Workflow (§§ 54–58, 67–69 + Schedules B & E)

Use this guide for **foundation selection and retaining-wall scoping**
in Yellowknife. Pinned-to-bedrock foundations are the local norm —
the by-law provides prescriptive paths that avoid needing an RDP for
common Part 9 cases, with Schedule B drawings for piers and Schedule E
drawings for retaining walls.

---

## Foundations — §§ 54–58

### §54 — Continuous concrete foundation pinned to bedrock (Part 9, no RDP)

**Scope:** Part 9 buildings only. May be approved without requiring an
RDP if the following prescriptive requirements are met:

| Clause | Requirement |
|---|---|
| §54(1) | **20 mm rebar dowels at max 1200 mm o/c** OR 15 mm rebar dowels at max 900 mm o/c. Dowels grouted ≥ **300 mm into bedrock** and extended ≥ 300 mm above bedrock. |
| §54(2) | **10 mm rebar** secured to each dowel, extending vertically to within 150 mm of the top of the concrete wall. |
| §54(3) | **10 mm horizontal rebar** at max 600 mm o/c. Bottom row within 300 mm of bedrock; top row within 200 mm of top of concrete. **Minimum 2 rows required.** |
| §54(4) | **Maximum wall height — 3.66 m.** |
| §54(5) | Concrete slabs within the foundation shall be supported by **compacted granular fill to bedrock**. |

§55 — all other aspects of concrete foundations shall conform to the
by-law.

### §56 — Concrete pier foundation pinned to bedrock (Part 9, no RDP, per Schedule B)

A concrete pier foundation pinned to bedrock for a Part 9 building
**may be approved without requiring an RDP** if designed in
conformance with **Schedule B** of the by-law.

Schedule B (pp. 29–32) provides four prescribed cases:

| Case | Building dimensions | Pier spacing | Max axial load | Max lateral load | Pier dia. | Max pier height | Reinforcement |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** (p. 29) | 13.2 × 15.15 m | 2,400 mm (8 ft) | 630 kN (142 kips) | 4.5 kN (1 kip) | 300 mm (12") | 2,400 mm (7'-10½") | 4 × 15M longitudinal + 10M ties @ 190 o/c (150 o/c top/btm 600 mm) |
| **2** (p. 30) | 13.2 × 15.15 m | 3,000 mm (10 ft) | 800 kN (180 kips) | 6.3 kN (1.4 kips) | 300 mm (12") | 2,130 mm (6'-11⅞") | 4 × 15M + 10M ties @ 190 o/c (150 o/c top/btm 600 mm) |
| **3** (p. 31) | 14.15 × 14.15 m | 4,500 mm (15 ft) | 1,175 kN (264 kips) | 9.75 kN (2.2 kips) | 300 mm (12") | 1,760 mm (5'-9 5/16") | **6 × 15M** + 10M ties @ 150 o/c |
| **4** (p. 32) | 13.2 × 15.15 m | 6,000 mm (20 ft) | 1,822 kN (410 kips) | 21 kN (4.7 kips) | 300 mm (12") | 1,410 mm (4'-7½") | **6 × 30M** + 10M ties @ 150 o/c |

Common reinforcement detailing across all cases:

- **Dowels epoxied into bedrock** — 300 mm (12") embed. Check epoxy
  manufacturer's manual.
- **Lap length for dowels and longitudinal rebars** — 1,200 mm (4 ft)
  tied to longitudinal rebars. (Drill and epoxy the longitudinal
  rebars into bedrock as dowels **whenever possible**.)
- **Concrete** — minimum **30 MPa** at 28d, 5–8% air, **40 mm cover**.
- **Exposed bedrock** required at founding.

### §57 — Surface Mount Foundation (narrow case, no RDP)

Permitted without RDP if supporting a **non-residential, one-storey
garage storage structure or accessory building** with total floor area
**< 55 m²**. Constraints:

- **Not masonry or masonry veneer construction.**
- Concrete slab specs: **30 MPa at 28d**, 5–8% air, **10M rebar @ 305
  mm (12") o/c both directions**, **40 mm cover**.

Acceptable foundation types: woodblocks, concrete pads, or minimum
100 mm concrete floor slab.

### §58 — All other foundation types

Everything outside §§ 54 / 56 / 57:

> All other foundation types shall be **designed in conformance with
> Part 4 of the N.B.C.** Plans and specifications shall be **prepared
> by an RDP** with applicable **letters of assurance** provided at
> the time of plan examination and field review reports as required
> by an Inspector.

### Decision tree — foundation selection

```
Building is Part 9?
├── Yes
│   ├── Solid bedrock + using prescriptive path?
│   │   ├── Continuous wall, ≤ 3.66 m → §54 prescriptive (no RDP)
│   │   ├── Piers matching Schedule B case → §56 + Schedule B (no RDP)
│   │   └── Neither → §58 (RDP + NBC Part 4)
│   ├── Non-residential accessory, 1 storey, < 55 m², not masonry
│   │   → §57 Surface Mount (no RDP)
│   └── Anything else → §58 (RDP + NBC Part 4)
└── No (Part 3 or larger)
    → §58 (RDP + NBC Part 4)
```

## Retaining walls — §§ 67–69 + Schedule E (pp. 37–39)

### Height-based thresholds

| Height | Supports structure? | Permit & design |
|---|---|---|
| **≤ 600 mm** | Not supporting | **No permit required** (§67) |
| **> 600 mm and < 1200 mm** | Not supporting | Designed to prevent sliding/overturning; **drawings** per Schedule E (§68) |
| **≥ 1200 mm** | Any, OR supporting any structure | **RDP-designed**, stamped/sealed structural drawings + letters of assurance (§69) |

### Schedule E — prescribed design summaries (pp. 37–39)

Schedule E offers three pre-designed retaining-wall types for walls up
to **1,200 mm** (i.e. the §68 range):

#### (1) Reinforced Concrete Retaining Wall (p. 37)

- Maximum height: **1,200 mm (not exceeding)**.
- Concrete: **25 MPa** compression strength at 28d; 5–8% air;
  aggregate 14–20 mm; **75 mm cover**.
- Reinforcement:
  - Vertical: **15M @ max 500 mm o/c**.
  - Horizontal: **15M @ max 500 mm o/c**.
  - Flexural: **15M @ max 305 mm o/c**.
  - Longitudinal at footing: min **2-15M @ max 450 mm o/c**.
  - **Min 750 mm lap length.**
- Footing: **min 610 mm** width.
- Min 100 mm Ø weeping tile.
- Min 600 mm layer of minus-50 mm compacted gravel under footing.
- Natural sandy soil backfill (recommended minus-50 mm gravel
  backfill).

#### (2) P.T. 6×6 Timber Wall (p. 38)

- Height: H = 1,219 mm (i.e. up to 1,200 mm range).
- PT 6×6 pieces with **9.5 mm (3/8") Ø rods**, min 254 mm (10")
  length, at 1,219 mm (4 ft) o/c — approximately 152 mm away from
  both ends of each piece.
- Tie-back configuration: min 800 mm long PT 6×6 connected to 6×6
  with min 2-9.5 mm rods with min 254 mm length; tie-back at H/3.
- **12.5 mm Ø rods** with 1,000 mm (40") length at 1,219 mm o/c, min
  152 mm from both ends, to secure to below.
- Min 100 mm Ø weeping tile; min 600 mm compacted minus-50 mm gravel
  layer.

#### (3) Allan Block Wall (p. 39)

- Height: H = 1,219 mm.
- Allan Block Wall: 200 mm H × 265 mm D × 460 mm L blocks filled with
  same soil as backfill.
- **3 layers of Geogrid** placed between blocks and into the backfill
  for 914 mm (3 ft).
- Min 100 mm Ø weeping tile; min 600 mm compacted minus-50 mm gravel
  layer.
- **Required:** 3 layers of Geogrid for lateral stability when wall
  is entirely vertical. Refer to manufacturers' manuals for Allan
  blocks and Geogrid details.

### Drafting guidance

- **Under 600 mm, no-permit threshold (§67)** applies **only if the
  wall doesn't support a structure**. Even a short wall supporting a
  deck post gets pulled into §69 (RDP-designed).
- **Between 600 mm and 1,200 mm (§68)** — the by-law requires
  drawings that show sliding/overturning resistance. Schedule E
  provides three acceptable typologies; adopting one (with site
  verification) is usually the cheapest path.
- **1,200 mm and above (§69)** — RDP-designed, no prescriptive
  option. Budget for structural engineering.

## Site realities

- **Bedrock exposure.** Yellowknife is on Canadian Shield — bedrock
  is often close to surface. Both §54 and §56 assume "pinned to
  bedrock" and require exposed bedrock at the interface. Site
  selection affects foundation choice early.
- **Frost penetration.** Check frost depth with the Inspector and
  your structural engineer; prescriptive §54 / §56 details are
  targeted at bedrock pinning, not frost-protected shallow
  foundations.
- **Epoxy quality.** Schedule B details explicitly tell you to
  "check the epoxy manufacturer's manual" — pick a dowel epoxy
  rated for cold-weather installation and grout it at temperatures
  the manufacturer allows.
- **Out-of-scope cases default to RDP.** The prescriptive sections
  are narrow. Anything unusual (slopes, mixed bedrock / soil,
  non-Part 9, supporting a Part 3 structure) → §58 RDP + NBC Part 4.
